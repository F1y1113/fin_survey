Abstract

Despite recent advances, large language models (LLMs) in finance face a persistent gap between research benchmarks and real-world requirements. This survey bridges the gap through a unified framework combining two perspectives. From a practitioner-centric view, we present a comprehensive taxonomy mapping five core financial functions—Data Analysis, Investment Research, Trading, Investment Management, and Risk Management—to key tasks, datasets, and constraints like regulatory compliance and data heterogeneity, offering actionable insights for practical deployment. From a research-focused view, we review cutting-edge LLM techniques, including retrieval-augmented models, instruction-tuned architectures, and multi-agent frameworks. We identify critical challenges in accuracy, interpretability, and adaptability, and propose solutions such as domain-specific fine-tuning, hierarchical agents, and dynamic market-aware benchmarks. Our work emphasizes modular, transparent LLM design and stronger researcher-practitioner collaboration to enable safer, scalable AI adoption in finance.
